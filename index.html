The Mayan Calendar

The Tzolk'in

The Tzolk'in combines 20 day signs with 13 tones, creating a 260-day sacred calendar that has guided seekers for millennia in understanding cosmic patterns and personal energy.

The Mayan Long Count

The Mayan Long Count is part of the classical Mayan Calendrical system rooted deeply in the Mayan Civilization. Historically used for tracking large cycles of time over thousands of years. Its starting point is 0.0.0.0.0 corresponding to August 11th 3114 BC, Gregorian Calendar.

The Dreamspell Count

The Dreamspell is a modern reinterpretation of the Tzolk'in with spiritual and harmonic goals. Created by Jose and Lloydine Arguelles, it began on July 26th 1987 aligning with the heliacal rising of Sirius.
