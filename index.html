The Mayan Long Count and the Dreamspell Count

The Mayan Long Count and Dreamspell Count are 2 distinct systems for measuring time within the MesoAmerican calendars. Both systems offer unique insights yet they differ in origin and structure.

The Dreamspell Count

Dreamspell: The Journey of Timeship Earth was designed by Jose and Lloydine Arguelles introducing new terminology and spiritual concepts not present in the traditional Mayan system. It became a cornerstone of the New Age Movement and gave rise to the Harmonic Convergence August 16th and 17th 1987.

It's a more idealized and non-linear perspective designed for spiritual exploration and global harmony. Its epoch began on July 26th 1987 aligning with the heliacal rising of Sirius.

The Mayan Long Count

The Mayan Long Count is part of the Classical Mayan calendrical system. It's epoch is 0.0.0.0.0. corresponding to August 11th 3114 BC (Gregorian Calendar) and September 6th 3114 BCE (Julian Calendar).

Rooted deeply in the Mayan Civilisation and historically used for recording dates in monuments and texts over thousands of years.
