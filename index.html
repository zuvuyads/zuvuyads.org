The Mayan Long Count and the Dreamspell Count

The Mayan Long Count and the Dreamspell Count are two distinct counts rooted in the Mayan Tzolkin yet they differ in structure and origin.

The Mayan Long Count

The Mayan Long Count is a part of the Classical Mayan Calendric system. Its epoch began on 0.0.0.0.0. corresponding to August 11th 3114 BC (Gregorian Calendar) and September 6th 3114 BCE (Julian Calendar).

Historically, the Mayan Long Count was used as a continuous calendar system to record exact dates across long periods of time.
The Maya documented dynastic events, monument dedications, rituals and mythic origins with a precision far beyond cyclical calendars.

The Dreamspell Count

Dreamspell: The Journey of Timeship Earth was designed by Jose and Lloydine Arguelles in 1990.

Introducing new concepts and terminology, the Dreamspell became a cornerstone of the New Age Movement, giving rise to the Harmonic Convergence (August 16th/17th 1987). It is a more idealized and non-linear perspective, designed for spiritual exploration and global harmony. Its epoch began on July 26th 1987, aligning with the heliacal rising of Sirius.

The heliacal rising of Sirius is an annual event and occurs at its first reappearance in the sky after a period of being hidden by the Suns glare.

How Zuvuya Came to Be

"The voyage of the best ship is a zig zag line of a hundred tacks."

— RW Emerson

I bought my first two astrology books at the Theosophical Bookstore on Capitol Hill in Seattle Washington.

Esoteric Astrology scribed by Alice Bailey and The Encyclopedia of Medical Astrology by H.L. Cornell M.D.

Anyone can read Esoteric Astrology. There are no glyphs, no symbols, no astrological equations, and it presents astrology in such a way as to turn most traditional views of astrology on its head. It was a good place to start.

This was not the case with the Encyclopedia of Medical Astrology.

The Encyclopedia is a mix of words, descriptions and astrological correlations none of which I had a clue about.

Seattle was my hometown, I knew the area well and went looking for an Astrology 101 class.

Astrology Et Al had a storefront in the University District. I was shown a list of the classes they were offering, and Jeff Green’s class caught my eye.

Before I bought my first two astrology books, I had been Gurdjieff-ing. My first husband and I were a part of a Gurdjieff group and coming from that background a class on Evolutionary Astrology was much more appealing than the 101 series.

I asked if I could sign up for Jeff's class and was discouraged from doing so. It was suggested that until I took a basic course it might be too difficult to understand.

I had no intention of following that suggestion. I showed up on the night of his class and asked if I could sit in even with my limited understanding of astrology. He said fine so I took a seat.

Jeff was 7 years away from catching the wave to fame. His classes were lightly attended. I asked a lot of questions that usually began with “What do you mean by.....”

We became a couple shortly after.

I attended numerous astrology conferences and met some very fine and diverse astrologers, but I was never drawn to become a practicing professional astrologer.

In 1985 Pluto: The Evolutionary Journey of the Soul was published. It was outstandingly well received. Jeff became in high demand as a speaker, the Pluto book was translated into several languages and became a fixture in the astrological hall of fame.

In 1988 we separated and I relocated to Northern California.

I was gifted The Dreamspell in 1993.
What I hadn’t been able to find thru Astrology I found thru the Dreamspell.

Nowadays we often hear the call to “remember” who we are. That we have both a family lineage and a cosmic or galactic lineage. Both Astrology and the Dreamspell offer a way to evoke this remembrance and validate experiences that arise from refined awareness.

By utilizing the familiar astrological template and repositioning planetary placements in relationship to the Mayan Tzolkin, a path is created for astrologers to glean insight from an alternative and harmonically based foundation.

It provides a unified framework for those drawn to the study of Astrology in search of their own unique insights and simplifies the complexity of the Dreamspell. By setting the foundation of the chart in accordance with the Mayan Tzolkin and not the time and place of birth, the Zuvuya House System is more suited and reflective of the movement of Pluto into Aquarius, Uranus into Gemini and Neptune into Aries.
